Reviews and advice on private entertainment talents

We are a Tripadvisor with ranked customer reviews on private entertainment talents, such as musicians, DJs, chefs and photographers. On Talentap.me and our mobile app, customers can a) find a talent by occa-sion, b) write and read authentic reviews on talents, and c) contact and book directly with the talent. Talents can showcase their services and promote their Talentap profile via social media. Advertisers can target customers with complementary services.

Information Architecture

Problem

Lack of centralized and ranked customer reviews on talents makes it difficult for customers to decide which talent to book. Most talents lack web presence and have low visibility on major search engines. Talents can't afford web optimization, SEO, Pay Per Click, or digital marketing.

Solution

Talentap provides review and advice on private entertainment talents, which will help customers to book the right talent. We use smart algo-rithms to rank and display talents. We consolidate once fragmented in-formation into a centralized database of talents. We have made it effort-less for talents to create a profile, promote their services, invite past cus-tomers to write a review, and monetize their services directly with the customer. It costs nothing for talents and customers to use Talentap. We take a small % on each booking.

Target Market

We target customers searching for private entertainment services, espe-cially around seasonal events, and sell eyeballs to advertisers. Demand for such services is evident, as over 800,000 Google searches are made using word combinations “find/book/rent/hire a photographer/DJ/ musi-cian/private chef”, monthly. In 2012, people spent $45bn on wedding and kids entertainment services. Industry reports do not disclose the market size of private entertainment services consumed by individuals and busi-nesses, due to lack of reporting. In 2013, total global ad spend was $503bn, with digital ad spend accounting for 21.8%, worth $109.7bn (Techcrunch).

Competitive Advantage

None of the competitors act as a Tripadvisor for talents in private enter-tainment. They are task-driven and charge high fees. At Talentap, cus-tomer reviews and talent ranking drive booking decisions. Talentap will outperform competition by empowering talents to monetize directly with the customer.

Marketing Strategy

Law of the Few (Gladwell): 1) The Saatchi Gallery Magazine will interview us, write about Talentap, and announce a talent competition in their May issue (Readership: 250k people per quarter); 2) Facebook invited us to their office last year and will support our launch by featuring Talentap on their page; 3) Jacqueline has been invited to talk about at TEDx London in March 2014; and 4) TNT Magazine booked talents with Talentap and has given us permission to use their logo for promotional purpose.

Word of mouth: Each of our talents will invite their customers to write a review, building their online and offline presence. Therefore, our talents will help growing Talentap through word-of-mouth, inherently.

Key partnerships: Collaborate and cross-promote with 1) online commu-nities, such as wedding blogs, mumsnet.com, saavyauntie.com and food blogs, 2) industry associations, such as art performing, photography and food associations, and government’s culture and sports department; 3) agencies, such as entertainment, recruitment and casting agencies; 4) ven-ue partners, such as HireSpace.com.

Search Engine Optimization: 1) Invite industry experts to guest blog on useful content using specific links, words and sentences to drive site traf-fic. 2) Empower talents with tips and tricks on how to drive traffic to their Talentap profile, such as video and picture name tagging, wordings of content, ratio of relevant links etc.

Promotional video: We would love to convince Adam Lisagor to create and feature in our promotional video. He’s the guru when dealing with start-up videos (http://sandwichvideo.com/).

Revenue Model

Three revenue streams: 1) advertisement including display advertising, sponsorships and partnerships, email, search and lead generation; 2) big data including monthly talent reports; and 3) premium talent accounts. Future revenue streams: 4) commission on talent booking and 5) research and big data.
